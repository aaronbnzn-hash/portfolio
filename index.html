Leveraging AI to deepen post-purchase retention and brand loyalty.

Duration 4 Months
Role Product Strategist

Capabilities Applied

The Outcome

01 The Challenge

Lenskart needed to move beyond transactional relationships to keep users engaged after their purchase. In a market saturated with options, the "one-and-done" purchase cycle was the primary barrier to sustainable growth. The goal was to transform a utilitarian eyewear brand into a lifestyle partner through intelligent touchpoints.

02 The Process

Data-Backed Framework Design

Analyzing cohort behavior to identify the exact moments when user interest begins to plateau.

AI-Tool Integration

Prototyping generative AI features for personalized styling advice and maintenance reminders based on lens type.

Retention Loop Mapping

Designing "Katana Path" workflows that connect every purchase to a future value-added interaction.

Market Research & Product Strategy for Amul Organic.

Synthesizing consumer demand and retailer adoption into a cohesive entry strategy for the organic dairy market.

The Challenge

Amul Organic faced a critical market entry problem: how to transition from a mass-market leader to a premium organic provider without alienating its core base. The goal was to identify high-value consumer segments and design a distribution model that ensured retailer shelf-space dominance in competitive urban markets.

The Process

Research Strategy

Deployed mixed-methods research, combining ethnographic store observations with large-scale quantitative surveys across Tier 1 cities.

Execution

Iterative focus groups were used to test packaging psychology, focusing on the visual cues that signal 'Organic' in the Indian context.

The Insight

Decision Making

Retailers prioritized shelf-life stability over organic certification, leading to a pivot in product cold-chain logistics strategy.

84% Retailer Alignment Score

Data Analytics

Correlation identified between high-end gym proximity and organic dairy churn rates.
